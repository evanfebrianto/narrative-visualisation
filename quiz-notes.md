## Week 9 Quiz Notes: Narrative Visualization

### 1. Visual Structure & Transitions

**Primary Goal of Visual Structure:** The primary goal is to **help the viewer navigate the scene**. It is intentionally designed to lead the viewer through the elements of the scene in a particular, structured order.

**Goal of Scene Transitions:** Transitions serve to **keep the viewer from becoming disoriented**. They help users retain a vital sense of context as data or charts change from scene to scene within a narrative visualization.

---

### 2. Narrative Visualization Structures

#### Martini Glass Structure

**Characterization:** A **slide show followed by free-form exploration**.

**How it works:** The initial slide show represents the "stem" of the glass, delivering tight, author-led content. This path later opens up into a wider, free-form exploration experience, which corresponds to the widening "bowl" of the glass.

#### Interactive Slideshow Structure

**Characterization:** A **slide show with the ability to drill down into the details in each slide**.

**How it works:** This structure maintains a main author-led pathway. However, it provides the viewer the flexibility to branch off into user-driven side paths to drill down into specific details on a slide (though these side paths are often author-led as well).

#### Drill-Down Story Structure

**Characterization:** A **single scene with the ability to initiate a slide show from select points in the scene**.
